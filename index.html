About Me

I am a UX designer who likes photography. I started photography in my sophomore year when I exchanged at Berkeley. My first camera was Sony A6000, and a year later, I switched to Sony A7R2. This self-taught experience is indeed a fantastic journey. During the last four years, my growth and memory have been well documented in my photos.

Photography cultivates me to be more detail-oriented and aesthetic. I also learned lots of photo editing skills during this journey. Photography is not only about the moment of taking photos; meantime, photo editing is identically essential. Color editing can 100% alter the vibe of photos. Object editing is also essential, especially for astrography and portrait photography. I recently learned how to replace skies, which is super helpful when cloudy skies ruin the scenery.

My travel philosophy is not to go to as many places as possible like many people. I think it isn't very meaningful to rush from one country to another. I don't enjoy commuting, and I don't want to waste my money on similar tourist experiences. I travel occasionally, but I try to make every trip unique and memorable. Compared to quantity, I focus more on quality. Even if it means spending more on a single trip, I don't want to compromise and get the worse ones, without seeing the most splendid sceneries.
